Chris C. Lim

Postdoctoral Associate @Yale University

I'm currently an assistant professor at the College of Public Health, University of Arizona. I'm interested in the relationship between the environment and human health and in potential application of sensors and wearables. I was a postdoctoral associate at Yale School of the Environment and graduated with a PhD from NYU School of Medicine.

Current Projects

Temperature and Mortality

Potential association between long-term temperature variability and mortality risk is examined in the U.S.

Greenspace and Health

I explore how neighborhood parks, trees, and greenspaces can affect our health.

Wearables for Asthma

State-of-art wearables are deployed to study real-time environment-asthma relationship in children.

Community Sensor Network

Air pollution prediction models will be developed using data from low-cost sensors installed throughout NYC.
