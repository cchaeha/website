Chris C. Lim

Assistant Professor @ The University of Arizona

I'm currently an Assistant Professor in the Department of Community, Environment, and Policy at the Mel & Enid Zuckerman College of Public Health, The University of Arizona. My research examines how environmental exposures influence human health via epidemiology, statistics, and data science approaches. I'm also interested in the potential applications of low-cost sensors and wearables.

I was recently a postdoctoral associate at the Yale School of the Environment (2019-2020) and graduated with a PhD from NYU School of Medicine in 2019.

Current Projects

Temperature and Mortality

Potential association between long-term temperature variability and mortality risk is examined in the U.S.

Greenspace and Health

I explore how neighborhood parks, trees, and greenspaces can affect our health.

Wearables for Asthma

State-of-art wearables are deployed to study real-time environment-asthma relationship in children.

Community Sensor Network

Air pollution prediction models will be developed using data from low-cost sensors installed throughout NYC.
